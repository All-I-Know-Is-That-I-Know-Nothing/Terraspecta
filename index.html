01 - About

Colour as a record of place

Color Loci began as a weekly ritual: photograph one stretch of south-east London, run the images through a colour pipeline, and watch a place change across a year in nothing but its palette.

This is the public version. Anyone, anywhere, can add the colours of their own view. Your photos are processed entirely on your device - only the extracted palette data is ever sent. The pictures themselves never leave your phone or computer.

Each contribution produces two palettes: a sky / earth split and a six-bin breakdown. Click any pin on the atlas to see that place's palettes in full.
